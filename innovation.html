Practice No. 1

From “we should do something with AI” to a plan you can defend.

Our workflow audits map repetitive tasks, bottlenecks, handoffs, and hidden cost centers — then rank where AI saves real time and money, with a tested prototype and the numbers to back the decision.

The audit, step by step

A fixed-length, fixed-price engagement. You know the cost and the deliverables before we start.

Map the terrain

Interviews across your team surface where time, money, and patience actually leak. We inventory your data, tools, and constraints while we’re at it.

Rank the opportunities

Every candidate idea gets scored on impact, feasibility, and risk — in a matrix your CFO will actually respect. Sacred cows welcome; most don’t survive.

Prototype the winner

We build a rough, working version of the top idea and put it in front of the people who would use it. Reality is the cheapest consultant you’ll ever hire.

Deliver the case

You get the prototype, the scorecard, a build-vs-buy recommendation, and a roadmap — whether or not we’re the ones who build it next.

Who it’s for

Leaders who are done with AI theater.

If you’ve sat through one more vendor demo, one more “AI strategy deck” with no line items, one more pilot that never left the lab — the sprint is the antidote. It’s opinionated, fast, and it ends in something you can touch.

We’re equally happy telling you not to build something. About a third of the ideas we test don’t clear the bar, and knowing that early is worth the sprint on its own.
